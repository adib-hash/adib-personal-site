## Design Context

### Users
Professional audience — operators, investors, collaborators — who may come across this site via LinkedIn, email intros, or word of mouth. They're evaluating whether Adib is someone interesting to work with or follow. The tone should feel personal and conversational, not corporate or performative.

### Brand Personality
Curious, creative, substantive. Not "developer portfolio." Not "LinkedIn-y." Closer to a thoughtful person sharing their work and thinking — part personal site, part intellectual home base.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial dark — warm, not cold. The gold accent (#d4a647) anchors the palette; all neutrals should be tinted toward it for cohesion. References: Notion and Claude/Anthropic (clean, intentional, generous whitespace). Anti-references: typical dev portfolio, startup landing page, corporate bio page. Dark mode is the correct choice for this audience and use context.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over coolness** — dark doesn't mean cold. Every surface should lean gold, not blue-gray.
2. **Typography carries the personality** — the font pairing is the loudest design decision. Serif headings with humanist grotesque body creates editorial feel without stiffness.
3. **Restraint on decoration** — the geometric accents (stars, corner brackets) are already the right amount. Don't pile on.
4. **Professional but human** — conversational tone in copy, enough negative space that nothing feels cramped, serif display type that reads like a byline not a resume.
5. **Mobile-first** — this is a PWA on an iPhone home screen. Every spacing/type decision should be validated at 390px width first.
